# Bewertungsraster / Grading Rubric — Zeugnis-Entwürfe

This rubric is anchored in Swiss law (Art. 330a OR and case law: **wahr, wohlwollend, vollständig, klar**; truth beats benevolence, BGE 136 III 510; no coded language), NOT in similarity to the five sample letters. A generated letter that differs stylistically from the samples but satisfies the legal principles scores well. A letter that mimics the samples but inflates a "gut" to "sehr gut" fails.

Score each dimension on Swiss school grades: **6** = excellent, **5** = good, **4** = sufficient, **3** = insufficient, **2** = poor, **1** = unusable.

## Dimensions and weights

| # | Dimension | Weight | What to check |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Calibration fidelity (no grade drift)** | 25% | For every competency row checked on the form, the letter's wording must sit at the same intensity level (sehr gut / gut / genügend) as the checkbox. Wording variation is fine; intensity change is not. Upgrading = untruthful (violates Wahrheitspflicht); downgrading = actionable by the employee. Unchecked rows must be OMITTED, not invented. Superlatives/"stets"/"jederzeit" only where "sehr gut" is checked. Score 6 = zero drift; each drifted dimension costs roughly one grade; any invented rating caps this at 3. |
| 2 | **Completeness (Art. 330a minimum content)** | 20% | Must contain: identity of employee, nature and duration of employment (function, entry date — or placeholder), main duties, assessment of performance (Leistung) AND conduct (Verhalten, incl. toward superiors/colleagues/customers if rated), correct issue location/date slot, two signatories. Final letters additionally: end date and exit statement. If the form itself lacks data, a placeholder counts as complete; silently missing elements do not. |
| 3 | **No invented facts / correct placeholder use** | 20% | Every factual claim (dates, birthplace, tasks, language skills, projects, pensum) must be traceable to the form or the company config. Missing data → bracketed placeholder, never a plausible-sounding value. Empty Hauptaufgaben → placeholder block, never an invented task list. Any single fabricated fact caps this dimension at 2 (this is the trust-killer for the product). |
| 4 | **Tense and Zeugnisart correctness** | 10% | Zwischenzeugnis: consistent present tense, "continued collaboration" closing. Arbeitszeugnis: consistent past tense, exit paragraph with date and legally appropriate exit phrasing (employer termination: neutral, no gratuitous reason; resignation: "auf eigenen Wunsch"; retirement/transfer handled correctly). Mixed tenses or wrong closing family = 3 or below. |
| 5 | **Clarity — no coded or ambiguous language** | 10% | No classic Zeugnis codes ("bemühte sich stets", "war stets gesellig", "im Grossen und Ganzen", "zeigte Verständnis für seine Arbeit"), no irony, no double meanings, no telling omissions presented as praise. Weak assessments must be expressed through openly modest-but-fair phrasing, not hidden signals. |
| 6 | **German language quality (Swiss conventions)** | 10% | Swiss standard German: **no ß anywhere**; dates as "1. Januar 2023"; correct and consistent gender forms (Frau/Herr, sie/er, possessives); professional register; no repetitive sentence patterns; grammatically clean. |
| 7 | **House-style structure match** | 5% | Paragraph order: intro → company paragraph → task list → performance → conduct → closing → place/date → two signatories (Vorgesetzte*r + HR). Deliberately the smallest weight: structural deviation is cheap to fix; the dimensions above are not. |

## Weighted total

`Total = Σ (grade × weight)`. Interpretation:
- **≥ 5.5** — production-draft quality; HR would edit lightly.
- **5.0–5.4** — usable draft; HR edits noticeably but saves time.
- **4.0–4.9** — draft has real defects; needs review of every sentence.
- **< 4.0** — fails; do not build Phase 1 on this without prompt changes.

**Hard-fail overrides (regardless of weighted total):** any invented personal data presented as fact; any grade drift on Leistung (the single most litigated sentence); coded language in a weak-employee letter. One hard fail = case fails.

## Comparing against a real HR letter (real cases only)

Categorize every difference between generated and real letter before judging it:

| Category | Treatment |
|---|---|
| **A. Agent error** | Wrong/missing content the form supports. Counts against the score. |
| **B. Unknowable enrichment** | Real letter contains data NOT on the form (HR-system dates, task lists, language skills). Generated letter must show a placeholder or omit — this counts FOR the agent, not against it. |
| **C. Real letter's own deviation** | The real letter itself departs from the form (e.g. upgrades a "genügend", uses a phrase from a different register). The generated letter following the FORM is correct; do not penalize divergence from the human letter here. |

The similarity question we actually care about: *would Sandra recognize this as a house-style draft she can finish?* — not *is it a byte-level clone?*

## Grading the Hinweise für HR section

Not weighted separately, but check: every placeholder in the letter must be listed; every ambiguity on the form (double-checks, empty rows, missing signature, Zeugnisart contradiction) must be flagged; no false alarms. Systematically missing Hinweise on a messy form = subtract 0.5 from the weighted total.
